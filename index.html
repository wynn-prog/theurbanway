About the Book

Cities are not just places—they are pathways.

The Urban Way explores how culture moves through urban life: through food, music, migration, and neighborhood change. Across global cities, everyday spaces become sites where ideas, identities, and traditions are constantly exchanged and transformed.

Blending sociology and storytelling, the book offers a new way of seeing the city—not as a fixed location, but as a dynamic process shaped by movement.

About the Authors

J.W. and A.D. are sociologists specializing in cities and culture. Their work examines how urban spaces shape identity, creativity, and social life.

He is the author of The Set Up, a critically engaged thriller, and teaches courses on urban sociology, media, and politics.

The Urban Way reflects his ongoing interest in how culture moves—and how cities make that movement visible.
